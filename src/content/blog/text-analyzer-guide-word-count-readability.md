## What Our Text Analyzer Measures

### Basic Counts
- **Words** — total word count (hyphenated words counted as one)
- **Characters** — with and without spaces
- **Sentences** — detected by punctuation (.!?)
- **Paragraphs** — separated by blank lines
- **Lines** — total line count

### Readability Scores
- **Flesch Reading Ease** — higher = easier to read (60-70 is good for web content)
- **Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level** — U.S. school grade equivalent (8-9 is good for general audience)
- **Reading time** — estimated time to read the full text
- **Speaking time** — estimated time to speak the text aloud

### Advanced Metrics
- **Keyword density** — how often each word appears
- **Average word length** — characters per word
- **Average sentence length** — words per sentence
- **Unique words** — vocabulary variety indicator

## Why Readability Matters for SEO

Google prioritizes content that users actually read and engage with. Readable content gets:

- **Lower bounce rates** — readers stay longer on easy-to-read pages
- **Higher dwell time** — a positive ranking signal
- **Better mobile experience** — shorter sentences work better on small screens
- **More social shares** — accessible content spreads further

## Writing Tips Based on Text Analysis

| Metric | Target (Web Content) | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Words per sentence | 15-20 | Short sentences are easier to scan |
| Flesch Reading Ease | 60-70 | Accessible to most adults |
| Grade level | 8-9 | Junior high reading level |
| Paragraph length | 2-4 sentences | Short paragraphs improve mobile readability |
| Keyword density | 1-3% | Enough for SEO, not enough for stuffing |
