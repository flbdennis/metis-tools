## What Is BMI and Why Does It Matter?

BMI measures the relationship between your height and weight, providing an estimate of body fat. While it's not a diagnostic tool, it's used by:

- **Healthcare providers** for initial health assessments
- **Insurance companies** for policy underwriting
- **Fitness professionals** to track client progress
- **Individuals** monitoring their weight management goals

## How BMI Is Calculated

The formula is straightforward:

- **Metric:** BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)
- **Imperial:** BMI = 703 × weight (lbs) / height² (in²)

## BMI Categories Explained

| Category | BMI Range |
|---|---|
| Underweight | Below 18.5 |
| Normal weight | 18.5 – 24.9 |
| Overweight | 25.0 – 29.9 |
| Obese | 30.0 and above |

Each category comes with different health implications. Your doctor can help interpret what your specific number means for you.

## Tips for Accurate BMI Measurement

1. **Use your current height and weight** — out-of-date numbers lead to inaccurate results
2. **Measure in the morning** — for the most consistent weight reading
3. **Know the limitations** — BMI doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition

## Beyond BMI

BMI is a screening tool, not a complete health assessment. Athletes with high muscle mass may register as "overweight" despite being in excellent shape. Always consult a healthcare professional for personalized health advice.
