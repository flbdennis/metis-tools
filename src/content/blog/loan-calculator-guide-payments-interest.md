## Key Loan Terms to Know

Before using a loan calculator, understand these terms:

- **Principal** — The amount you borrow
- **Interest Rate** — The annual cost of borrowing (APR)
- **Loan Term** — How long you have to repay (months or years)
- **Monthly Payment** — What you pay each month (principal + interest)
- **Total Interest** — The total interest paid over the loan's life

## Pro Tips for Loan Planning

1. **Compare APRs, not just monthly payments** — A lower monthly payment with a longer term often costs more in total
2. **Round up your payments** — Even $20 extra per month can save thousands in interest
3. **Check for prepayment penalties** — Some loans charge fees for paying off early
4. **Use the 28/36 rule** — Housing expenses should be under 28% of gross income; total debt under 36%

## Understanding Amortization

With most installment loans, early payments go mostly toward interest. As the loan matures, more of each payment goes to principal. Our calculator's amortization table lets you see this shift over time — a powerful insight for deciding when to make extra payments.
