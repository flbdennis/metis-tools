## 5 Percentage Calculations Everyone Should Know

### 1. X% of Y — "What is 20% of 150?"

The most common use case. Calculate discounts, tax amounts, or proportions. Example: What's 20% off a $150 item? Answer: $30 off ($120 final).

### 2. X Is What % of Y — "50 is what percent of 200?"

Great for calculating scores, ratios, or progress. Example: You scored 45 out of 60 on a test. That's 75%.

### 3. Percentage Change — "From 100 to 120 is what % change?"

Track growth, price changes, or performance. Example: Revenue grew from $10K to $12.5K — that's a 25% increase.

### 4. Percentage Increase — "Add 15% to 200"

Calculate prices after tax, markup, or tips. Example: A $200 item with 15% tax = $230.

### 5. Percentage Decrease — "Subtract 25% from 80"

Calculate sale prices or discounts. Example: 25% off $80 = $60.

## Quick Mental Math Tricks

1. **10% of any number** — move the decimal one place left (10% of 250 = 25)
2. **5%** — take half of 10% (5% of 250 = 12.5)
3. **15%** — add 10% + 5% (15% of 250 = 25 + 12.5 = 37.5)
4. **1%** — move the decimal two places left (1% of 250 = 2.5)
